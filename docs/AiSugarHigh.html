AI Sugar High

It's so sweet

Among the human fallacies is the "sugar high" effect that makes us fall for short term gains while ignoring long term costs.

We eat the sweets, enjoy the deserts, and consume the energy drink because it tastes so good and makes us feel energetic or "high".

While we grow up we have to learn how to restrain our sugar consumption, enjoying it as luxury but not as a general diet. This learning can fail for many reasons with severe health issues as the result.

Whoever helps you with this learning is not an anti-enjoyment puritan. It's somebody who cares for your general well-being.

The Code Generation High

When programming, we produce code, and that takes some effort. When GenerativeAI produces that code for us without much effort, it feels sweet.

"Look, Ma! No hands!"

The immediate benefits are apparent. We feel energetic, capable, embolden. We are the champions, the heros of a new age!

The AI Diabetes

Unfortunately, prolific use of code generation leads to considerably more code. That code is not only an asset. It is also a liability. It literally adds weight.

Larger codebases are more difficult to understand, more difficult to keep consistent, more difficult to secure, in short: more difficult to maintain. This is true even for high-quality code (if AI code generation ever reaches that level).

There are of course many more drawbacks that we have to expect. My goal is not to provide an exhaustive list of detriments here. My goal is to raise awareness to the human fallacy of falling for the perceived quick-win while ignoring the downstream damage.

Responsible Adults

We cannot expect the kids and teenagers to give up on sweets or to come up with a responsible arrangement on their own. This is up to the adults.

Unfortunately, there are many players in the game that are old enough to act like adults but with regard to the sugar-high fallacy behave like in adolescence.

I hope that by recognizing this fallacy, one or the other manager or engineer might organize for a responsible handling of the mighty machine.
