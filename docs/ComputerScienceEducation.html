Computer Science Education
"Education is what remains after you have forgotten everything you have learned."
quoted by Werner Heisenberg

Education as knowledge transfer

It is the obligation of the old generation to educate the next one.

This is expensive and cumbersome and often frustrating and at the very least uncomfortable for everybody.

But the point is to preserve any acquired knowledge and to pass it on such that we do not repeat old mistakes - but make new and better ones.

People stand in the way

So why even bother with these unreliable, forgetful, ever-revolving students at all? It would be so much more efficient to directly train the machines! Just put all the knowledge in the AI model and skill files and MCP server or whatever codec is currently required. If we delegate our decisions to the machines anyway, why not eliminating the "middle man/woman"?

This thought experiment immediately feels wrong (if not, see a doctor). We educate for responsible decision-making, not for execution performance. It takes the experience of human life to be a doctor or a judge or a nursery worker. The same is true for an engineer who builds and operates the technical infrastructure that our modern civilization depends upon.

This is where education becomes more than training: we need to consider how our activities effect peoples lives with all their needs and longings and weaknesses and hopes. We are responsible for our fellow neighbors but in a wider sense also for preserving the basis of our very existence and a liveable future for our children (e.g. use resources wisely, avoid toxic waste, do not destroy society).

By the people, for the people

"A student is not a vessel to fill but a torch to light up."
Albert Einstein

Teaching about sophisticated, profitable topics might be the apparent function of a university, but it is not the ultimate purpose.

The purpose is to facilitate a journey for our students to become inspired, independent, competent, responsible people that perform their occupation not merely as a job, but as a profession.

We cannot teach this via video tutorials or online classes. No "AI-tutor" can do this. We need to re-discover the "universitas magistrorum et scholarium", the community of students and teachers, where we work as a team, collectively overcome obstacles, and share success.

In the best of cases, students might find a role model for professional conduct, dependability, curious attitude, ethical behaviour, speaking the truth, or simply for how to stay human in tumultuous times.
